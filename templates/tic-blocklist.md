# Tic Blocklist (Template)

> **Purpose (hard-won lesson):** The same style tics returned chapter after chapter. This **living list** starts (almost) empty and grows as soon as a tic is caught *once* — after that the Writer watches for it while writing and the Editor while reviewing. Lives at `style/tic-blocklist.md`.
>
> **Rule:** Single occurrences are allowed. **Serial clustering** is the error. Diegetically necessary occurrences are protected (`open-points/diegesis-protection-register.md`) and do not count as a tic.

## Active Tics (avoid)

| ID | Pattern | Why it's a problem | Allowed exception |
|---|---|---|---|
| TIC-01 | {{routine word field X, in rows}} | marker clustering, reads mechanically | {{canonical definition at passage Y}} |
| TIC-02 | {{closing anaphora *"In the A … In the B …"*}} | predictable chapter close | — |
| TIC-03 | {{slash classes in prose (*X/Y/Z*)}} | labels instead of prose | {{genuine diagnostic blocks}} |
| TIC-04 | {{recurring sentence construction *"in which …, in which …"*}} | mannered when clustered | — |

## Typical Candidates (generic — make concrete on first appearance)

- Narrator aphorisms at the end of a scene/chapter
- Adjective / rule-of-three chains as a style reflex
- The same image/metaphor in consecutive chapters
- Routine verbs identical across characters instead of the voice signature

## Workflow

1. Editor or Reader reports a clustering → make it concrete here as a new entry.
2. The Writer gets the list on every run; watches for it **proactively** while writing.
3. Diegetically necessary occurrences → into the "allowed exception" column **and** into the diegesis-protection-register, so a later remediation pass does not break them.
