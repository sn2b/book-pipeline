# Diegesis Protection Register (Template)

> **Purpose (hard-won lesson):** A cast-wide word-field remediation pass broke a canonical, diegetically necessary form — only the verification pass caught it. Here, passages deliberately left sparse/empty/"wrong" are tagged **at the moment they are created**, so no later remediation/tic pass "corrects" or "fills" them. Lives at `open-points/diegesis-protection-register.md`.
>
> **Rule:** Before every word-field/tic pass, the conductor hands the relevant entries here to the Writer. These passages are **off-limits** for automatic remediation.

## Workflow

1. Writer deliberately sets a sparse/empty/atypical passage → enter it here **immediately**.
2. Author waves through a deliberately "unremediated" passage → enter it here (with date).
3. Conductor before every remediation pass: relevant entries go into the protection brief for the Writer.
4. The verification pass additionally checks whether a pass violated a protected passage.
