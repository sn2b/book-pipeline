# Questions — Author Vision (Template)

> **The highest authority in the entire project.** All worldbuilding and every canon decision serves these answers. These files are **never changed** by the agents — only by the author.
>
> **Fill this out first, before anything else.** Create a separate file per topic under `questions/` (e.g. `questions/protagonist.md`). Answer in full sentences — these are your own notes about the material, not keywords for later.
>
> **Two layers:** the topic clusters below are the *scaffold* (which files to create); the **99 interrogation prompts** in the appendix are the *depth* (what to answer to fill them). Work the appendix, capture the answers in the cluster files.

## Recommended Files & Guiding Questions

### beginning-and-end.md
- Where does the book begin, where does it end? What feeling should the first / last sentence leave behind?

### protagonist.md
- Who is the main character? What do they want, what do they need? What is their inner lack?

### antagonist.md
- Who/what works against them? Motive, means, weakness. Is it a person, a system, the world itself?

### characters.md
- Ensemble: roles, relationships, functions. Who changes, who doesn't?

### conflict-and-tension.md
- Central conflict. Which ticking clocks? What is at stake?

### plot-and-structure.md
- Rough act structure. Turning points. What is mandatory, what is negotiable?

### world-and-setting.md
- Where/when does it take place? What rules apply? How hard are they?

### tone-genre-atmosphere.md
- Genre (mix). Core tone. Comparison titles. What should it NOT be?

### themes-and-originality.md
- What is it about beneath the surface? What is the original twist?

### twists-and-revelations.md
- Planned twists, revelations, sacrifices. What may the reader know, and when? (Couples to `canon/knowledge-reveal-tracker.md`.)

### personal.md
- Why this book? What do you want to discover while writing it?

---

> **Note to the agents:** In a conflict between a `questions/` answer and a worldbuilding file, `questions/` always wins. Conflicts are reported, not silently resolved.
