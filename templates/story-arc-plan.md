# Story Arc Plan — {{BOOK_TITLE}} (Template)

> **Hard-won lesson (why this file exists):** On an earlier book, the shape of the whole story was discovered *while drafting* — the ending, the midpoint mechanic, the antagonist's real nature and the protagonist's arc only clicked into place after several chapters were already written, which forced backfilling and rewrites. This template exists so the **whole arc is planned, and its load-bearing decisions resolved, BEFORE Chapter 1 is drafted.**
>
> **Gate:** No chapter gets written until this plan exists, the **ending is decided**, and every decision this plan marks *load-bearing* is at least 🟡 in `canon/canon-decision-register.md`. This is checked in the **Story-Level Pre-Flight** (see `templates/pre-flight-checklist.md`).
>
> **This is a living document.** It may change as chapters teach you things — but it changes *deliberately*, via a decision, not by drift. Keep it reconciled with the Canon Decision Register, the Chapter Synopsis Register, and the Setup-Payoff Register. Lives at `story/story-arc-plan.md`.

## 0. Plan-before-prose order of operations

Work top to bottom. Do **not** start prose until step 6 is green.

1. **Vision** — the author's answers in `questions/` exist and are read.
2. **Ending decided.** Write the last beat first (see §2). Everything aims here.
3. **Premise mechanics locked.** The core rule / conceit and its hard limits are decided (they gate everything).
4. **Arc beat sheet** filled in (§3): act by act, with the midpoint and both turns.
5. **Owed-payoff ledger** (§5) started: every promise the opening makes has a scheduled payoff.
6. **Load-bearing decisions** (§6) are all at least 🟡 in the Canon Decision Register.
7. **Lore/worldbuilding + character arcs** (§7) sketched enough that Chapter 1 will not contradict the midpoint or ending.
