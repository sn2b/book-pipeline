# Complexity Budget (Template)

> **Purpose (hard-won lesson):** Trying to lay additional meaning/form layers (e.g. a nested sub-logic of in-world rules) onto already dense passages lowered clarity **without** emotional gain and had to be reverted. This budget sets a deliberate ceiling. Lives at `style/complexity-budget.md`.

## Base Rule

> **One clear layer beats three stacked ones.** Before you add another layer of meaning, form, or mechanics to a block: *does this complexity earn itself?*

## Budget per Block / Scene

- **At most {{N}} simultaneously active layers of meaning** on a climax/key block. (Suggested starting value: 2.)
- A new layer **only** if it (a) adds something emotionally and (b) the reader can resolve it without a footnote.
- On a block that already carries a resolution/punchline, **do not** stack a second concept layer.

## The "does it earn itself?" test (3 questions)

1. **Can the text carry it?** Is the block calm enough for another layer — or already dense?
2. **Does the reader gain something?** Emotion/tension/clarity — or just authorial elegance?
3. **Can the reader resolve it?** Without foreknowledge from later chapters, without reading backwards?

Two "no"s → drop the layer or move it to a calmer block.

## Escalation

If the Writer deems a layer necessary but the reader rates it with low **C (≤ 3)** → the layer is a **suspected over-layering**. Default: revert, resolve the cross-reference implicitly. Note it as a lesson in the bookkeeping.
